Data Entrepreneurship in Action (DEiA, JM0201-M-6) is the final course in the In Action series, following Data Intrapreneurship (JM0120-M-6) and Data Consultancy (JM0130-M-6). While the previous courses focus on innovation and problem-solving within existing companies, DEiA guides students through the end-to-end process of launching a new data-driven venture, from team formation and idea generation to securing funding and pitching to investors.
Students begin by learning how to form an effective founding team. Working in small, newly-formed co-founding teams, they then navigate the critical early stages of launching a new data-driven venture, whether based on a fresh idea, one developed during the Master’s program, or an idea they have long wanted to pursue. During the ideation stage, students also learn how to use recent advances in GenAI as a co-creative partner to generate and refine ideas, as well as identify promising venture opportunities. As the course progresses, students focus on developing and validating their venture model, exploring funding strategies, and preparing a persuasive investor pitch. The course culminates in the creation of a professional venture pitch deck and a final pitch to a panel of investors. At every stage, students integrate theoretical and empirical knowledge into hands-on practice. This evidence-based approach not only prepares students to launch new ventures but also equips them with adaptable entrepreneurial skills that are equally valuable in established companies.
Experiential learning is a central pillar of the course. Students interact with a diverse group of decision-makers throughout the different stages of the venture creation process. During workshops and progress presentations, they receive feedback from instructors and peers that supports critical reflection and further development of their ideas. In business coaching sessions, (former) entrepreneurs and professional investors serve as mentors, offering practical guidance drawn from their real-world experience. Former alumni also share personal lessons learned from both successful and unsuccessful ventures, giving students a well-rounded and realistic understanding of the entrepreneurial journey.
Many of these interactions take place in close collaboration with the JADS Playground. The JADS Playground also remains available after the course for students who wish to continue developing their ventures beyond the classroom.
